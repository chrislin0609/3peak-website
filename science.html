The Science

The research behind the sachet.

This page is where we document the probiotic strains, clinical studies, and third-party testing behind 3PEAKS Hydration+ - so nothing in the formula is a black box.

Probiotic Strains

What's actually in the blend.

3PEAKS Hydration+ is formulated with two clinically studied, human-origin probiotic strains - both isolated from the gut of an Olympic weightlifting gold medalist.

01

Lactobacillus plantarum PL-02

BCRC 911012 = CGMCC 20485. In a 6-week randomized clinical trial, PL-02 supplementation significantly increased muscle mass (p < 0.05) and blunted the rise in muscle-damage and inflammation markers after intense exercise.1

02

Lactococcus lactis subsp. lactis LY-66

BCRC 911055 = CGMCC 21838. In the same trial, LY-66 supplementation significantly increased VO2max and explosive power, and significantly reduced body fat percentage (p < 0.05).1

Why we use them together

In the same trial, the combined PL-02 + LY-66 group improved across every measured pillar - VO2max, explosive power, and post-exercise recovery - while also significantly increasing Akkermansia muciniphila, a gut bacterium linked to metabolic health (p < 0.05).1

Dosing, Transparently

Studied dose vs. our serving size.

We formulate with the same two strains used in the trial below, but our serving size is our own - here's how they compare.

🧪

As studied in the trial

7.5 billion CFU per sachet, 2 sachets/day (~15 billion CFU/day), dissolved in 250mL water, alongside structured exercise training, for 6 weeks minimum.

Clinical Research

The trial behind the formula.

One randomized, double-blinded clinical trial spans all three of our performance pillars - here's what it found, by pillar.

After 6 weeks, VO2max increased by 6–9% in the PL-02, LY-66, and PL-02+LY-66 groups (p < 0.001 vs. baseline for each), with no significant change in the placebo group.1

PL-02 significantly increased muscle mass (p < 0.05). Across all probiotic groups, strength loss after exercise-induced muscle damage was significantly smaller, recovery at 24–48 hours was faster, and the rise in muscle-damage marker creatine kinase and inflammatory marker IL-6 was significantly blunted (p < 0.05–0.001).1

Across countermovement jump, isometric mid-thigh pull, and Wingate anaerobic tests, all probiotic groups showed significant increases in force output and power (4–9%, p < 0.01–0.001) after 6 weeks, versus no meaningful change in the placebo group.1

Source

1 Lee, M.-C.; Hsu, Y.-J.; Chen, M.-T.; Kuo, Y.-W.; Lin, J.-H.; Hsu, Y.-C.; Huang, Y.-Y.; Li, C.-M.; Tsai, S.-Y.; Hsia, K.-C.; et al. "Efficacy of Lactococcus lactis subsp. lactis LY-66 and Lactobacillus plantarum PL-02 in Enhancing Explosive Strength and Endurance: A Randomized, Double-Blinded Clinical Trial." Nutrients 2024, 16, 1921. https://doi.org/10.3390/nu16121921 - open access, randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial, n=88, registered at clinicaltrials.gov (NCT06092723), IRB-approved (Landseed International Hospital, LSHIRB No. 21-042-A2).

Disclosed in the published paper: several study authors are employed by Glac Biotech Co., Ltd., which supplies the PL-02 and LY-66 strains. We're noting that here in the interest of the same transparency we ask of our own labels.
